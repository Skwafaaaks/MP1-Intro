What is a tattoo?

Tattoos are a form of body art that involve the insertion of ink or pigments into the skin's dermal layer to create permanent designs or images. They have been practiced for thousands of years and are culturally significant in many societies around the world

History and Techniques

Tattoos have a rich and diverse history. They have been found on mummies dating back to ancient Egypt (around 2000 BC) and have been a part of various indigenous cultures' traditions for centuries. Tattoos have served as symbols of status, identity, and spirituality in different societies.

Tattooing techniques have evolved over time. Traditional methods involve using needles attached to a handheld tool to manually puncture the skin and deposit ink. Modern tattoo machines use electromagnetic coils to drive needles rapidly in and out of the skin. Tattoo artists carefully control the depth, speed, and pressure to create precise designs.
